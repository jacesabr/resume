Apriori Algorithm

Frequent itemsets and association rules — the foundation of market basket analysis and recommendation systems.

20 examples across 2 datasets · Individual code snippets · LaTeX equations

Mathematical Foundation

\[\text{Support}(A) = \frac{|\{T:A\subseteq T\}|}{|T|}\]

Fraction of transactions containing itemset A

\[\text{Confidence}(A\Rightarrow B) = \frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(A)}\]

Probability of B given A was purchased

\[\text{Lift}(A\Rightarrow B) = \frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(A)\cdot P(B)}\]

Lift>1: positive association. Lift=1: independent.

\[\text{If } A \text{ infrequent} \Rightarrow \text{all supersets infrequent}\]

Anti-monotone property prunes search space

Glossary of Technical Terms

Transaction
A shopping basket / purchase event.
Itemset
Set of items. {bread, milk} is a 2-itemset.
Support
How often itemset appears. 0.05 = 5% of transactions.
Confidence
P(right side | left side purchased).
Lift
How much more likely vs random chance. 3.0 = 3× more likely.
min_support
Threshold for frequent items. Too low=noise. Typical: 0.01-0.05.
Candidate Generation
Building (k+1)-itemsets from frequent k-itemsets.
Frequent Itemset
Appears in ≥ min_support fraction of transactions.

import pandas as pd
from mlxtend.frequent_patterns import apriori, association_rules
from mlxtend.preprocessing import TransactionEncoder

import pandas as pd
df = pd.read_csv('Groceries_dataset.csv')
transactions = df.groupby(['Member_number','Date'])['itemDescription'].apply(list).tolist()

# --- DATA EXPLORATION ---
print(f'Total transactions: {len(transactions):,}')
print(f'Average items per transaction: {sum(len(t) for t in transactions)/len(transactions):.1f}')
print(f'\nSample transactions:')
for i in range(min(3, len(transactions))):
    print(f'  Transaction {i+1}: {transactions[i][:6]}')

# Find most popular items
from collections import Counter
all_items = [item for t in transactions for item in t]
top_items = Counter(all_items).most_common(10)
print(f'\nTop 10 most purchased items:')
for item, count in top_items:
    print(f'  {item:30s}: {count:5d} times ({count/len(transactions)*100:.1f}% of transactions)')

# Convert to binary matrix
te = TransactionEncoder()
basket = pd.DataFrame(te.fit_transform(transactions), columns=te.columns_)
print(f'\nBinary matrix: {basket.shape[0]} transactions × {basket.shape[1]} unique items')

# --- MINE ASSOCIATION RULES ---
freq = apriori(basket, min_support=0.02, use_colnames=True)
print(f'\nFrequent itemsets found: {len(freq)}')

rules = association_rules(freq, metric='confidence', min_threshold=0.3)
print(f'Association rules found: {len(rules)}')

# --- RESULTS: Top rules ---
top = rules.sort_values('lift', ascending=False).head(8)
print(f'\nTop rules by {mname.lower() if mname.lower() in (__STR0__,__STR1__,__STR2__) else __STR3__}:')
for _, row in top.iterrows():
    ant = ', '.join(list(row['antecedents']))
    con = ', '.join(list(row['consequents']))
    print(f'  IF buys [{ant}] → THEN also buys [{con}]')
    print(f'    Confidence: {row[__STR0__]:.1%} (of people who buy A, this % also buy B)')
    print(f'    Lift: {row[__STR0__]:.2f}× (how much MORE likely than random chance)')
    print(f'    Support: {row[__STR0__]:.3f} ({row[__STR1__]*100:.1f}% of all transactions)')
    print()

# --- METRIC EXPLANATIONS ---
# SUPPORT: How often items appear together. Support=0.05 means in 5% of transactions.
# CONFIDENCE: P(B|A) — if someone buys A, probability they also buy B.
#   Confidence=0.6 means 60% of A-buyers also buy B.
# LIFT: Confidence / P(B). Lift>1 means A increases likelihood of B.
#   Lift=3.0 means buying A makes buying B 3× more likely than random.
#   Lift=1.0 means no association. Lift<1.0 means negative association.

# ✓ VERDICT: Classic market basket — top rules
